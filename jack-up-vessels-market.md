<p><ul><li>Self-propelled Jack-up Vessel</li><li>Normal Jack-up Vessel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Jack-up vessels are categorized into two primary markets: self-propelled jack-up vessels and normal jack-up vessels. Self-propelled jack-up vessels have their own propulsion systems, allowing them to navigate without the need for tugboats, enhancing operational efficiency and flexibility. In contrast, normal jack-up vessels rely on external tugboats for navigation, limiting their operational independence. Both types are primarily used in offshore drilling and construction, with specific market demands driving their respective applications and development within the maritime industry.</p></p>
