<p><p>The nasolacrimal stents market is primarily categorized into monocanalicular and bicanalicular stents. Monocanalicular stents consist of a single passage, facilitating tear drainage from the eye to the nasal cavity, often used for less complex obstructions. In contrast, bicanalicular stents feature two channels, allowing for improved drainage by accommodating both canaliculi. These stents are essential in treating nasolacrimal duct obstructions, with applications in ophthalmology to restore proper tear flow, improving patient comfort and quality of life.</p></p>
