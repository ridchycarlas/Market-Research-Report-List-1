<p><p>The carbon composite material market includes various manufacturing processes, notably Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation. CVD involves depositing carbon-rich gases onto a substrate at high temperatures, creating strong, uniformly dense composites ideal for aerospace applications. Liquid Impregnation, on the other hand, involves saturating a fibrous material with a liquid resin, which then cures to form a lightweight, durable composite. Both methods cater to industries requiring high-performance materials with specific mechanical and thermal properties.</p></p>
