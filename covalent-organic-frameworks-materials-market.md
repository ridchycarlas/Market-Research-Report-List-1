<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) materials can be categorized into two types based on their dimensional structure: two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D). </p><p>2D COFs are flat, layered structures that offer high surface area and accessibility for applications in catalysis and gas storage. In contrast, 3D COFs feature interconnected frameworks that provide enhanced stability and robustness, making them suitable for applications in drug delivery and separation technologies. Both types exhibit tunable properties, facilitating their use in various advanced applications.</p></p>
