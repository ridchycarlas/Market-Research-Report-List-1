<p><ul><li>Oncolytic Virotherapy</li><li>Gene Transfer</li><li>Gene-Induced Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cancer gene therapy encompasses various approaches to treat cancer through genetic modifications. Oncolytic virotherapy utilizes genetically engineered viruses to selectively target and kill cancer cells while sparing normal tissues. Gene transfer involves inserting genes into cancer cells to restore normal function or promote cell death. Gene-induced immunotherapy stimulates the immune system to recognize and attack cancer cells by enhancing immune responses. Together, these strategies represent innovative avenues for more effective and personalized cancer treatments, improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
