<p><p>Waste food management in commercial kitchens includes various methods to handle food waste efficiently. Aerobic digestion involves the breakdown of organic waste by microorganisms in the presence of oxygen, converting it into compost or soil amendments. In contrast, anaerobic digestion occurs in oxygen-free environments, where bacteria decompose organic material, producing biogas and digestate. Both methods help reduce landfill waste, lower greenhouse gas emissions, and generate valuable by-products, making them essential for sustainable practices in the foodservice industry.</p></p>
